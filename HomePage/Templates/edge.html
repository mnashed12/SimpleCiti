Powering The Future

What Are GPU Infrastructure Assets?

GPUs are now high-performance compute units powering the AI economy. Deployed in purpose-built infrastructure (“bare metal”), they become monetizable, yield-generating assets.

  • Bare metal GPU infrastructure provides compute power to AI developers, quant funds, and labs
  • Offers 15–20% cost savings and better security, latency, and flexibility vs. cloud platforms
  • Like data centers and solar farms, GPU clusters can be leased, collateralized, and valued on contracts and utilization rates

A Rapidly Scaling Market

The GPU-as-a-Service (GPUaaS) market — GPU processing by the hour — is growing fast.

  • Market projected to hit $205.7B in 2024 and $770B by 2030
  • Growth driven by foundation models and enterprise AI adoption
  • GPUaaS CAGR projected at 29.9% over 6 years
  • Infrastructure demand exceeds supply, creating durable investment potential

Institutional Relevance

With yield contracts, long lives, and tax benefits, GPU infrastructure is emerging as a new real asset class.

  • GPUs can be income-generating assets, like aircraft leases or cell towers
  • Hosting/power costs are known; revenues are fixed or indexed — supporting stable IRRs
  • SimpleEDGE builds GPU portfolios backed by contracts and infrastructure — turning chips into cash-flowing assets

Market Opportunity

SURGING DEMAND FOR AI COMPUTE

  • Global demand for GPU infrastructure is surging as developers race to build, train, and deploy next-gen AI models.
  • AI workloads — from training to inference and edge deployment — are increasingly GPU-driven and compute-intensive.
  • NVIDIA’s B200 GPUs, its most powerful general-purpose AI chips, are already oversubscribed across major hyperscalers.
  • As model complexity grows (e.g., GPT-5, Gemini, Claude), access to top-tier GPUs is becoming essential infrastructure.

BOTTLENECKS CREATE MONETIZABLE SCARCITY

  • Hyperscalers now retain 85–90% of next-gen NVIDIA GPUs for internal use and top-tier AI clients.
  • In 2024, over $75B in compute demand went unmet due to chip supply and power bottlenecks.
  • Institutional AI users outside top cloud channels now face 6–12 month wait times.
  • Spot rental platforms (Voltage Park, io.net) run > 90% utilization and charge 2–5x standard OEM rates.
  • This scarcity lets operators like SimpleEDGE lease compute to underserved segments — capturing yield in real time.
