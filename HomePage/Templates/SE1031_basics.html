1031 Exchange Fundamentals

Master the strategic tax-deferral vehicle that has empowered real estate investors for decades. Understanding the rules, requirements, and opportunities that define successful exchanges.

What is a 1031 Exchange?

A 1031 Exchange, under Section 1031 of the Internal Revenue Code, allows investors to defer capital gains taxes by reinvesting the proceeds from the sale of one investment property into another "like-kind" property. This strategy helps preserve equity, compound growth, and optimize after-tax performance over multiple investment cycles.

Key Benefits

Tax Deferral

Defer capital gains and depreciation recapture taxes to preserve investment capital for reinvestment.

Portfolio Growth

Reinvest pre-tax proceeds into larger or multiple properties to accelerate wealth building.

Diversification

Gain exposure to different property types, geographic regions, and market sectors.

Passive Ownership

Access professionally managed real estate through other institutional structures.

Estate Planning

Potential step-up in basis at inheritance can eliminate deferred tax liabilities for heirs.

IRS Rules & Requirements

1

Like-Kind Property

Must be real estate held for investment or business use. Personal residences do not qualify.

2

45-Day Identification

Replacement properties must be properly identified in writing within 45 days of sale.

3

180-Day Exchange

Replacement property must be acquired and closed within 180 days of initial sale.

4

Qualified Intermediary

A third-party QI must hold proceeds and facilitate the exchange to maintain tax deferral.

5

Equal or Greater Value

To defer all taxes, reinvest all sale proceeds and replace any existing debt obligations.

Investment Structures

Delaware Statutory Trusts

Passive fractional ownership of institutional-grade real estate with professional management and no landlord duties. Ideal for investors seeking hands-off real estate exposure.

Tenants-in-Common

Direct fractional ownership with shared rights and responsibilities among multiple investors. Offers more control but requires active participation in major decisions.
